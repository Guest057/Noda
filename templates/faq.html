FAQ

Welcome to our FAQ section! Here, you’ll find answers to the most common questions about our platform and how it works. Whether you’re curious about analyzing YouTube videos, exploring subtitles, or discovering how AI-generated answers can enhance your learning experience, this page has you covered. If you don’t see your question here, feel free to reach out – we’re here to help!

How does the platform retrieve subtitles from YouTube videos?

+ -
  • YouTube does not allow direct subtitles from videos and blocks such activity. Therefore, the video is first downloaded and then analyzed by AI. This allows you to get subtitles but gives a certain delay depending on the length of the video.

    • What types of questions can I ask about a video?

    + -
  • You can ask any questions related to the material presented in the video. And even if your question is not related to the video - Shea will simply not answer you and will suggest other topics for discussion.

    • Can I upload my own videos, or is it only for YouTube links?

    + -
  • We do not currently have such a feature. But our system foresees such a possibility in the future.

    • How accurate are the AI-generated answers?

    + -
  • Considering that the answer is given based on the video. The accuracy of the answer depends on the material that was provided. But if you encounter artifacts in the answer - contact tech support

    • What languages are supported for subtitle analysis?

    + -
  • We support almost all languages. But it is important to note that the best result will be provided using English. Also, the AI ​​will provide the answer in English - you need to specify the language in which you want to receive the answer.

    • Is there a limit to how many questions I can ask per video?

    + -
  • There is no limit to the number of questions you can ask. You are only limited by your imagination and purpose.

    • Why is the site so slow sometimes?

    + -
  • Our project is at an early stage. Right now we don't have large capacities, so we are using the most economical solutions for our resource. In further development, everything should improve.
